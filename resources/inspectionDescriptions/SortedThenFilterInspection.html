Take the following example: You have a list of 100 unsorted numbers and you want to have at the end a sorted list of prime numbers.
Let's assume that in the list are 20 even numbers.
If you first sort the list and then filter it, you will sort 100 numbers and than search for the prime ones.
But if you switch the order of sort and filter operations (filter first and then sort), in the end you'll sort only 20 numbers.
